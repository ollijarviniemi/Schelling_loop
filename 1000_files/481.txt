Gridley–Howe–Faden–Atkins Farmstead

The Gridley–Howe–Faden–Atkins Farmstead, also known as Brookside Farm, in Kimball County, Nebraska near Kimball, is a historic, well preserved farmstead. It has buildings and structures dating from 1899 when Henry H. Howe built a one-story limestone house until 1947 when the last structure on the property was built. The property claim had been proven by James Gridley in 1891, at which time the property was irrigated, but Gridley moved on to Utah, and Howe obtained the farm. Stone farm house, built in 1899 It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1997. The listing included seven contributing buildings and four contributing structures on .
